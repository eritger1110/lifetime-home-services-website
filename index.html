Radon Testing Services

Professional radon detection to protect your family's health

What is Radon?

Radon is a naturally occurring radioactive gas that comes from the breakdown of uranium in soil, rock, and water. It's invisible, odorless, and tasteless, making it impossible to detect without proper testing equipment.

Health Risks

Radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, responsible for approximately 21,000 deaths annually. The EPA estimates that 1 in 15 homes has elevated radon levels.

Our Testing Process

We use EPA-approved continuous radon monitors that provide accurate, real-time measurements over a minimum 48-hour period. Our certified technicians place monitors in the lowest livable level of your home for the most accurate results.

Understanding Results

  • Below 2 pCi/L: Low risk, no action needed
  • 2-4 pCi/L: Consider mitigation
  • Above 4 pCi/L: EPA recommends mitigation
  • Above 10 pCi/L: Immediate action recommended

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will this yellow in sunlight like old epoxy?

No — our polyaspartic top coat is UV stable and specifically designed to withstand garage door exposure.

Q: Will hot tires peel the coating?

No. We use true 100% solids epoxy as a base coat with the flakes and a polyaspartic topcoat, which chemically bonds to concrete and won't lift from heat transfer.

Q: Can it be done in one day?

Technically, yes — but we don't believe in rushing the job. A true chemical bond needs full cure time before sealing.

Q: Is it slippery?

Our flakes naturally add grip. We also offer anti-slip additives for extra traction if needed.

Q: How long does it last?

It will last the life of the concrete. But, if the concrete moves the floor coating will move with it. But, with basic care many customers never have to recoat.

Q: How do I clean it?

Just hose off, mop, or use a mild degreaser. It's sealed, stain-resistant, and waterproof.
