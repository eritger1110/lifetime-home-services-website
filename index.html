Professional Radon Testing

Protect your family with accurate, professional radon testing services

What is Radon?

Radon is a colorless, odorless, radioactive gas that occurs naturally when uranium in soil, rock, and water breaks down. It's the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, responsible for about 21,000 deaths each year.

Why Test for Radon?

The EPA recommends that all homes be tested for radon, regardless of geographic location or foundation type. Radon can enter your home through:

  • Cracks in foundation walls and floors
  • Gaps around service pipes
  • Construction joints
  • Crawl spaces
  • Well water (in some cases)

Our Professional Testing Process

1

Initial Consultation

We assess your home's structure and determine the best testing locations and methods.

2

Professional Testing

We use EPA-approved continuous radon monitors for accurate 48-72 hour testing periods.

3

Detailed Report

You receive a comprehensive report with results and recommendations within 24 hours.

Understanding Radon Levels

Radon is measured in picocuries per liter (pCi/L). The EPA action level is 4.0 pCi/L, meaning if your home tests at or above this level, you should consider radon mitigation.

Radon Level Guidelines:

  • Below 2.0 pCi/L: Low risk, no action needed
  • 2.0 - 4.0 pCi/L: Consider mitigation, especially for long-term exposure
  • 4.0 - 8.0 pCi/L: Mitigation recommended
  • Above 8.0 pCi/L: Immediate mitigation strongly recommended

Why Choose Professional Testing?

While DIY test kits are available, professional testing offers several advantages:

  • More accurate continuous monitoring equipment
  • Proper placement and environmental controls
  • Immediate results and expert interpretation
  • Compliance with real estate transaction requirements
  • Detailed reporting for insurance and mitigation planning

When to Test

The EPA recommends testing your home:

  • Every 2 years as part of routine home maintenance
  • Before buying or selling a home
  • After any structural changes to your home
  • If you're finishing a basement or lower level
  • After installing a radon mitigation system (post-mitigation testing)

Professional Radon Mitigation

Expert radon reduction systems to protect your family's health

What is Radon Mitigation?

Radon mitigation is the process of reducing radon gas concentrations in your home to safe levels. Our certified professionals install systems that prevent radon from entering your home and remove existing radon gas.

How Radon Mitigation Works

The most common and effective method is Active Soil Depressurization (ASD), which works by:

  • Creating a vacuum beneath your home's foundation
  • Drawing radon gas from the soil before it enters your home
  • Venting the gas safely above your roofline
  • Continuously operating to maintain protection

Our Professional Installation Process

1

Site Assessment

We evaluate your home's foundation, HVAC system, and optimal fan placement locations.

2

System Design

Custom system design based on your home's unique characteristics and radon levels.

3

Professional Installation

Expert installation with minimal disruption to your home and daily routine.

Types of Mitigation Systems

Active Soil Depressurization (ASD): The most common method, using a fan to draw radon from beneath the foundation.

Passive Systems: Rely on natural air pressure differences, often used in new construction.

Crawl Space Ventilation: For homes with crawl spaces, increasing air circulation to reduce radon buildup.

Sump Pump Systems: Sealing and venting sump pump systems that can be radon entry points.

System Components

  • Radon Fan: High-quality, continuous-duty fans designed for 24/7 operation
  • PVC Piping: Durable piping system to transport radon gas safely outside
  • Manometer: Monitoring device to ensure system is functioning properly
  • Warning Labels: Required safety labels and system identification
  • Electrical Components: Proper electrical connections and safety switches

Effectiveness and Maintenance

Professional radon mitigation systems are highly effective, typically reducing radon levels by 80-99%. Most systems reduce levels to below 2.0 pCi/L.

System Maintenance:

  • Check manometer monthly to ensure proper operation
  • Listen for fan operation (should run continuously)
  • Annual professional inspection recommended
  • Post-mitigation testing within 30 days of installation
  • Re-test every 2 years to ensure continued effectiveness

Cost and Energy Efficiency

Radon mitigation systems are designed for continuous operation with minimal energy consumption. Most systems use less electricity than a 100-watt light bulb, costing approximately $3-7 per month to operate.

AeroSeal Duct Sealing

Revolutionary technology to seal your ductwork from the inside out

What is AeroSeal?

AeroSeal is a revolutionary duct sealing technology that seals leaks from the inside of your ductwork. Think of it like this: imagine your ductwork is like a garden hose with holes in it. Traditional methods try to find and patch each hole from the outside. AeroSeal works from the inside, automatically finding and sealing every leak, no matter how small or hard to reach.

The AeroSeal Process

1

Pre-Sealing Measurement

We measure exactly how much air is leaking from your ducts using computer-controlled equipment.

2

Sealing Process

Safe, non-toxic particles are blown through your ducts, automatically sealing leaks from the inside.

3

Real-Time Results

Watch on our computer screen as leaks are sealed in real-time, with before and after measurements.

How It Works - The Science

AeroSeal uses a patented process that:

  • Temporarily blocks your vents and registers
  • Pressurizes your duct system with safe, non-toxic particles
  • Forces particles to stick only at leak sites (not on duct walls)
  • Builds up layers until leaks are completely sealed
  • Provides real-time computer monitoring of the sealing process

Measurable Benefits

Typical Results:

  • Energy Savings: 20-30% reduction in heating and cooling costs
  • Leak Reduction: 85-95% of duct leakage eliminated
  • Comfort Improvement: More even temperatures throughout your home
  • Air Quality: Reduced dust, allergens, and pollutants
  • HVAC Lifespan: Extended equipment life due to improved efficiency

Who Benefits Most from AeroSeal?

Homes with:

  • Rooms that are too hot or too cold
  • High energy bills
  • Excessive dust
  • Ductwork in unconditioned spaces (attics, crawl spaces)
  • Older homes (built before 2000)
  • Recent HVAC equipment replacement

Safety and Environmental Impact

AeroSeal is completely safe for your family and the environment:

  • Non-toxic, water-based formula
  • Same base material used in chewing gum and hair spray
  • No harmful chemicals or fumes
  • UL tested and approved
  • Reduces your home's carbon footprint

The AeroSeal Advantage

Unlike traditional duct sealing methods, AeroSeal:

  • Seals leaks you can't see or reach
  • Provides measurable, guaranteed results
  • Requires no demolition or ductwork access
  • Completes most homes in 4-6 hours
  • Comes with a 10-year warranty

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will this yellow in sunlight like old epoxy?

A: No — our polyaspartic top coat is UV stable and specifically designed to withstand garage door exposure.

Q: Will hot tires peel the coating?

A: No. We use true 100% solids epoxy as a base coat with the flakes and a polyaspartic topcoat, which chemically bonds to concrete and won't lift from heat transfer.

Q: Can it be done in one day?

A: Technically, yes — but we don't believe in rushing the job. A true chemical bond needs full cure time before sealing.

Q: Is it slippery?

A: Our flakes naturally add grip. We also offer anti-slip additives for extra traction if needed.

Q: How long does it last?

A: It will last the life of the concrete. But, if the concrete moves the floor coating will move with it. But, with basic care many customers never have to recoat.

Q: How do I clean it?

A: Just hose off, mop, or use a mild degreaser. It's sealed, stain-resistant, and waterproof.

  • Avoid harsh acidic cleaners
