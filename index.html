Radon Testing

Protect your family with accurate radon level detection

Why Radon Testing Matters

Radon is a colorless, odorless radioactive gas that can seep into your home from the ground. It's the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, responsible for about 21,000 deaths annually.

Our Testing Process

We use EPA-approved testing methods to provide accurate radon level measurements:

  • Professional-grade continuous radon monitors
  • 48-72 hour testing period for accurate results
  • Detailed report with recommendations
  • Follow-up consultation included

When to Test

  • Before buying or selling a home
  • After home renovations
  • Every 2 years for ongoing monitoring
  • If you've never tested your home

Understanding Your Results

The EPA recommends taking action if radon levels are 4.0 pCi/L or higher. However, any level of radon poses some risk, and the EPA also recommends considering mitigation for levels between 2.0 and 4.0 pCi/L.

What Happens Next?

If elevated radon levels are detected, we'll provide you with detailed information about mitigation options and can connect you with our certified radon mitigation specialists.

Professional Equipment

We use state-of-the-art continuous radon monitors that provide real-time data and tamper-evident features. Our equipment meets all EPA protocols and provides the most accurate results possible.

Health Benefits

Regular radon testing is one of the most important steps you can take to protect your family's health. Early detection allows for effective mitigation, significantly reducing your family's risk of radon-related health issues.

AeroSeal Duct Sealing

Revolutionary air sealing technology that seals your home's ductwork from the inside out

What Is AeroSeal?

AeroSeal is a breakthrough air sealing technology that seals your home's ductwork from the inside out using a patented aerosolized polymer. It finds and seals leaks inaccessible by hand, with up to 95% leak reduction — all measured and verified in real time.

Unlike traditional methods that apply tape or mastic to the outside of ducts, AeroSeal uses a computer-controlled process that targets and plugs leaks where they actually occur — on the inside of joints and seams.

Why Duct Sealing Matters

Most homes — even brand-new builds — leak 20% to 40% of their heated or cooled air through gaps in the ductwork. That means:

  • Wasted energy and high utility bills
  • Uneven room temperatures
  • Increased dust, allergens, and pollutants
  • Premature wear on your HVAC system

According to ENERGY STAR and the U.S. Department of Energy: "Duct leakage can reduce HVAC efficiency by as much as 40%, even in new homes."

The "Hose Analogy" – Why Even New Homes Need AeroSeal

Builders often use metal ductwork connected by crimp joints and sheet metal screws, then seal with foil tape. But:

  • Imagine attaching two garden hoses without a rubber gasket — no matter how tight they're twisted, water sprays out.
  • Now imagine taping around the outside of those hoses — it wouldn't stop the leak.
  • AeroSeal is the rubber gasket for your duct system — sealing from the inside, right at the source of the leak.

Even the most meticulously built homes have metal-on-metal duct joints that leak — often in attics, basements, crawlspaces, or behind walls where manual sealing is impossible.

Our Professional 3-Step AeroSeal Process

1. Prep & Pressurize

  • We temporarily seal off all registers and vents.
  • The duct system is isolated from the HVAC unit.
  • A diagnostic blower pressurizes the ducts.
  • A computer measures the exact amount of leakage in real time.
  • You see a "before" leakage report — usually between 20% and 40% leakage, even in new homes.

2. Seal with AeroSeal

  • We inject non-toxic polymer particles into the pressurized ducts.
  • As air escapes through gaps, particles stick to the edges of leaks.
  • Over 60–90 minutes, leaks close — from pinholes to ⅝ inch wide.

The sealant is:

  • Water-based and safe for pets and children
  • UL listed and GreenGuard Gold certified
  • Designed to target only leaks, not coat the inside of ducts

3. Verification & Certification

  • After sealing, we re-run the diagnostics.
  • Most homes see a leakage drop of 85–95%.
  • You receive a Certificate of Completion, showing:
    • Before/after leakage in CFM and %
    • Estimated annual savings
    • Final system tightness rating
  • Great for resale, documentation, and proof of energy upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this yellow in sunlight like old epoxy?
No — our polyaspartic top coat is UV stable and specifically designed to withstand garage door exposure.
Will hot tires peel the coating?
No. We use true 100% solids epoxy as a base coat with the flakes and a polyaspartic topcoat, which chemically bonds to concrete and won't lift from heat transfer.
Can it be done in one day?
Technically, yes — but we don't believe in rushing the job. A true chemical bond needs full cure time before sealing.
Is it slippery?
Our flakes naturally add grip. We also offer anti-slip additives for extra traction if needed.
How long does it last?
It will last the life of the concrete. But, if the concrete moves the floor coating will move with it. But, with basic care many customers never have to recoat.
How do I clean it?
Just hose off, mop, or use a mild degreaser. It's sealed, stain-resistant, and waterproof.

Maintenance & Warranty

  • Sweep or rinse regularly
  • Avoid harsh acidic cleaners
