What It Is (Explain Like I'm 10)

Radon mitigation is like installing a special ventilation system that pulls radon gas out from under your house before it can get inside. Think of it as giving your home a way to "breathe out" the bad air.

The most common system is called sub-slab depressurization—we create a small hole in your basement floor, connect a pipe that goes up through your house and out the roof, and add a quiet fan that pulls the radon out and sends it safely into the outdoor air.

The Bottom Line: It's a proven system that typically reduces radon levels by 80-99%. Most installations take one day, and the system runs quietly in the background.

How Mitigation Systems Work

Sub-Slab Depressurization System

The system creates negative pressure under your foundation, preventing radon from entering your home and safely venting it outside.

Installation Process

Our Professional Installation

1
Site Assessment & Design

2
Suction Point Creation

We carefully create a suction point in your basement floor, ensuring proper placement and minimal disruption to your space.

3
Pipe Installation

We install the vent pipe system from the suction point up through your home and out the roof, following all building codes.

4
Fan & Electrical

We install the radon fan (typically in the attic or outside) and connect all electrical components with proper safety measures.

5
Testing & Verification

We test the system operation and schedule follow-up testing to verify the system is effectively reducing radon levels.

System Types

Sub-Slab Depressurization

Most Common: Creates suction under the foundation to prevent radon from entering. Works for most basement and slab-on-grade homes.

  • Single or multiple suction points
  • Pipe routed through interior or exterior
  • Fan placement in attic or outside

Crawl Space Ventilation

For Crawl Spaces: Increases air circulation or creates suction to reduce radon accumulation in crawl space areas.

  • Passive or active ventilation
  • Plastic sheeting installation
  • Exhaust fan systems
